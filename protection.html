Protection

The FERPA act grants rights to the student. One of the rights is, the right to see information that the institution has kept on the student. Another is, the right to seek amendment to those records and in certain cases append a statement to the record. Also the right to consent to disclosure of their record. Finally, the right to file a complaint with the FERPA Office in Washington. These rights happen for a student upon enrolling in an institution of higher education. These rights belong to the student regardless of age. The records have on them personal information enrollment records grades and schedules.

FERPA gives certain rights to the parents of students that relates to their children's education records. Including personal information given during registration and even behavioral records. These rights transfer to the student when they turn eighteen or if they attend a school beyond the high school level. The students that have had these rights transferred to them are called eligible students. Parents and eligible students have the right to review the students education records that are obtained from the school. Some schools may charge a fee to review these documents and schools are not required to provide copies of records. Parents and eligible students have the right to request that a school correct records that they believe to be inaccurate. If the school does not fix the records then the parents and or eligible student have the right to a formal hearing. The main protection FERPA gives you is that schools are required to have written permission from the parent or eligible student to be able to send out any information from any of the students education record.
