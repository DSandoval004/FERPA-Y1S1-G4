History

Since April 2003, school nurses and school health officials have been claiming for the Family Education Rights Privacy Act (FERPA) interface in the school environment. FERPA provides students with protection regarding their private information. FERPA can protect students by giving limited access to their records and any other information the school may have. With the attention and security given to personally identifiable health information in all settings, it is imperative for school nurses, school administrators, and school attorneys to revisit policies and procedures for protecting the privacy of student and family health information in schools.

In 1974 congress passed the FERPA bill that changed the way students are protected and how parents can access the confidential records of their kids. This law affected every student in the United States. This bill was first talked about by Senator James Buckley. He was the reason this law passed and is now helping protecting student’s information. Senator Buckley pointed out many key issues that violated the students privacy. FERPA helped protect these students from identity theft and other crimes. Buckley made many key arguments that changed how schools handled the confidential documents that belonged to students. Senator wanted to protect student’s information. He then went on to present his legislation on the floor of the Senate. After Senator heard all of the concerns people had with the bill he made amendments to it. The FERPA bill became effective in November of 1974.
